## 1. Project Overview

Flying Tulip is a full-stack on-chain financial system founded by DeFi pioneer Andre Cronje, designed to unify spot trading, derivatives, lending, and stablecoin settlement within a single capital-efficient architecture. The protocol operates across Ethereum, Base, Avalanche, BNB Chain, and Sonic networks, positioning itself as infrastructure for the emerging "Agentic Economy." This approach theoretically reduces capital fragmentation and oracle dependency risks that plague modular DeFi stacks.

## 2. Product & Technical Stack

### Unified Financial Architecture

Flying Tulip's technical stack represents a significant departure from conventional DeFi design:

Depth-Aware Pricing Engine:

- Blends constant-sum and constant-product AMM curves using regime signals (EMAs over price/dispersion)
- Maintains both Time-Weighted Average Price (TWAP) and Reserve-Weighted Average Price (RWAP) windows
- Downstream systems (lending, futures, liquidations) use these executable liquidity feeds rather than external oracles [Docs](https://docs.flyingtulip.com/overview/flying-tulip/)

Cross-Margin Collateral System:

- Single deposit can simultaneously accrue yield, back orders, and secure futures positions
- Permissionless lending markets auto-deploy with every spot pair
- Permissioned pool enables advanced strategies like total return swaps with near-zero collateral paths

Oracle-Minimized Design:

- Futures settle to internal trading activity with sub-second updates
- Eliminates oracle lag/manipulation risk class
- Funding rates link directly to actual borrowing costs in lending markets

### ftUSD Settlement Layer

The native stablecoin ftUSD operates with unique mechanics:

| Feature             | Implementation                                                                     | Benefit                                         |
| ------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| Stability Mechanism | Delta-neutral yield strategies (e.g., supply USDC→Aave, borrow Sonic "S", stake S) | Reduces directional risk while generating carry |
| Yield Optionality   | Unstaked ftUSD: non-yielding, composable                                           | Pure stability for payments/settlement          |
|                     | Staked as sftUSD: accrues distributed yield                                        | Opt-in yield without locking periods            |
| Transparency        | Fully on-chain strategies, parameters                                              | Verifiable reserves and operations              |

The system routes yield from unstaked ftUSD to protocol operations and buybacks, while stakers receive distributions from the yield pool. [Docs](https://docs.flyingtulip.com/product-suite/ft-usd/)

### Risk Framework

The protocol emphasizes defense-in-depth with several innovative protections:

Circuit Breaker System: Rate-limits abnormal outflows with configurable thresholds
Segregated Redemption Reserve: Protects ftPUT holders' principal through dedicated on-chain collateral
Soft Liquidations: Time-sliced, depth-aware unwinds that route through spot markets
Conservative Sizing: Maximum 5% allocation to any single yield strategy venue [Docs](https://docs.flyingtulip.com/risks/)

### Revolutionary ftPUT Mechanism

The Perpetual PUT option represents Flying Tulip's most innovative tokenomic feature:

Three Operational States:

1. Hold: Maintain redemption right while participating in FT appreciation
2. Exit: Burn FT to reclaim original contributed asset at par value (e.g., 10,000 FT → 1,000 USDC)
3. Withdraw: Invalidate PUT option, releasing reserved collateral for protocol buybacks

This structure creates a programmatic price floor while allowing upside participation. As Andre Cronje stated, "Flying Tulip FDV is not standard FDV... closer to a NAV valuation than FDV." [The Defiant](https://thedefiant.io/news/markets/andre-cronje-flying-tulip-token-trades-near-usd1b-fdv-floor)

### Value Accrual Mechanics

Revenue Sources:

- Yield spread from deployed capital (currently primarily Aave strategies)
- Protocol fees from trading, lending, futures, insurance
- ftUSD carry generation

Capital Flow:

1. Primary contributions deployed to conservative yield strategies (Aave, Lido, etc.)
2. Yield covers operations and infrastructure costs first
3. Excess yield funds continuous FT buybacks
4. PUT invalidations release additional buyback capital

The model aligns incentives by funding development through yield rather than token inflation or continuous fundraising.
