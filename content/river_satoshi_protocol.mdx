## 2. System Architecture and Chain Abstraction Design

### Omni-CDP Module Architecture

River's core innovation lies in its Omni-CDP (Collateralized Debt Position) module, which leverages LayerZero's OFT (Omnichain Fungible Token) standard to enable native cross-chain minting without traditional bridges. The system operates through three key components:

1. Cross-Chain Collateral Management

- Users deposit BTC, ETH, BNB, or LSTs on a source chain (e.g., Ethereum)
- Collateral remains locked on the original chain, eliminating bridge custody risks
- LayerZero messaging coordinates debt positions across chains in real-time

1. Native satUSD Minting

- satUSD mints directly on the destination chain without wrapped assets
- OFT standard ensures uniform value and transferability across all supported networks
- No slippage or third-party bridge dependencies

1. Automated Position Management

- `_lzSend` and `_lzReceive` functions manage cross-chain debt synchronization
- Oracle-driven rebalancing maintains collateral ratios during market volatility
- Permissionless liquidation system activates below 110% collateralization ratio

## 3. Stablecoin Design and Asset Connectivity

### satUSD Mechanics

Collateralization Framework:

- Minimum Collateral Ratio (MCR): 110% for BTC, 160% for higher-risk LSTs
- Liquidation Threshold: Positions become liquidable below MCR
- Minting Fee Structure: Base rate + 0.5% (minimum 0.5%, maximum 5%)
- Interest Rate: Currently 0% for BTC collateral, variable for other assets

Peg Maintenance Mechanisms:

Hard Peg: Direct redeemability for underlying collateral provides a price floor. satUSD holders can always redeem for collateral value, preventing significant downside deviation.

Soft Peg: When satUSD trades above $1.10, arbitrageurs can deposit collateral at 110% ratio, mint satUSD, and sell in the market, increasing supply and restoring peg equilibrium.

Dynamic Fee Adjustment: Base rate adjusts based on redemption activity:

```
BaseRate_t = BaseRate_{t-1} × δ^Δt + 0.5 × (redemption_amount/total_supply)
```

Where δ = 0.944 (12-hour half-life decay factor) [River Minting Docs](https://docs.river.inc/products/editor/minting)

### Cross-Chain Consistency

River's OFT implementation ensures satUSD maintains identical properties across all chains:

- Unified Supply: Total satUSD supply tracked globally via LayerZero messaging
- Consistent Pricing: Oracle feeds provide uniform price data across chains
- Native Transferability: No wrapped versions or liquidity pools required for cross-chain movement

Current satUSD supply stands at approximately 31M tokens, with 95.7% concentrated in a single Ethereum address, indicating early-stage liquidity distribution. [Moralis](https://developers.moralis.com)

## 4. Opportunity Routing and Yield Logic

### Clarification on "Opportunity Routing"

Contrary to initial expectations of a dynamic on-chain routing engine, River's "Opportunity Routing" is currently implemented through managed vault strategies rather than autonomous cross-chain yield optimization. The system operates through two primary mechanisms:

Smart Vault (Retail Focus):

- One-click deposits automatically mint and stake satUSD
- Dual revenue streams: asset strategy yields + protocol revenue sharing
- Zero liquidation risk through automated rebalancing
- Governance-determined staking factors (0-100%) optimize risk-return profiles

Prime Vault (Institutional Focus):

- Custodial asset protection with leading institutional partners
- Internal satUSD minting never reaches user wallets
- Zero liquidation risk through custodial locks and automated position management
- Currently holds ~$250M in institutional deposits

### Yield Generation Logic

Primary Yield Sources:

1. Protocol Revenue Sharing: satUSD staking pools distribute trading fees and minting revenues
2. DeFi Strategy Deployment: Assets deployed to Morpho, Pendle, and other yield protocols
3. CeDeFi Arbitrage: Institutional-grade strategies managed by custodial partners

Oracle Integration: Every contract interaction triggers real-time price updates to maintain optimal collateral ratios and yield allocation, though specific routing decisions remain off-chain and governance-managed. [Smart Vault Docs](https://docs.river.inc/products/smart-vault)
