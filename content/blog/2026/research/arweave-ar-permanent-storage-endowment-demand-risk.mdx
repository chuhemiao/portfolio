## Pre-screen Decision

Full research. Arweave is foundational decentralized storage infrastructure and a major missing Research Map entry. It deserves a full memo because its value capture is more concrete than many infra tokens, but its current market cap is far below prior-cycle highs.

## TL;DR

Arweave is a permanent-storage network and permaweb protocol. Users pay once to store data permanently, while AR token economics support miner incentives and an endowment model. The official project is at [Arweave](https://arweave.org/), and the public implementation lives at [ArweaveTeam/arweave](https://github.com/ArweaveTeam/arweave). As of June 28, 2026, Surf shows AR around $1.84, with about $121.0M market cap/FDV and about $5.0M 24h volume.

Bottom line: Arweave is one of the higher-quality DePIN/storage assets. The risk is not fake utility; the risk is whether permanent storage demand grows fast enough to justify the token relative to cheaper storage, DA, and centralized alternatives.

## Project Overview

Arweave uses a blockweave architecture and Proof-of-Access mining to incentivize permanent data storage. Its ecosystem includes the permaweb, developer tooling, permanent apps/content, and adjacent compute narratives such as AO. Surf tags it as DePIN, Layer1, and storage/compute.

## Research Question

Is AR a durable monetary asset for permanent storage, or is the market overestimating demand for one-time permanent data relative to cheaper alternatives?

## Architecture and Mechanism

Users pay upfront for permanent storage. The network allocates fees between immediate miner incentives and long-term endowment assumptions intended to keep data available over time. This is a differentiated model: unlike recurring cloud bills, Arweave sells long-duration persistence. AR value capture is tied directly to storage demand and miner economics.

## Economics and Value Capture

AR has relatively clear value capture: users need AR-denominated economics to store data, miners earn incentives, and the endowment model links network usage to long-term data availability. The open question is volume of permanent storage demand. If AI data, provenance, NFTs, app frontends, and archival use cases grow, AR can matter. If most data stays on cheaper mutable systems, AR remains a niche asset.

## Red-team Check

The strongest bear case is that permanent storage is important but not large enough to support a major token economy. The most gameable metric is ecosystem announcement count without paid storage growth. The value-capture failure path is that apps use Arweave through subsidized abstractions without sustainable AR demand. The zero path is a storage-cost/endowment failure or a superior storage network capturing the market.
