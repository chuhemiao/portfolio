## TL;DR

- **Verdict:** Velvet is a selective AI / DeFi trading-infrastructure watchlist, not high-conviction allocation yet.
- **Why it matters:** It sits in a useful category: non-custodial onchain trading terminal, AI copilot, multi-chain routing, perps, yield, and vault-style portfolio tooling.
- **Main risk:** The product can be useful while the token still has weak value capture. VELVET needs real trading volume, fee capture, staking demand, and retention beyond launch incentives.

## Executive Summary

Velvet Capital is an AI-powered onchain trading terminal. The official site describes it as a non-custodial DeFi trading platform for spot, perpetuals, and yield across seven chains: Base, Ethereum, BNB Chain, Solana, Hyperliquid, Monad, and Sonic. It integrates routing through major aggregators and protocols, including Jupiter, 1inch, 0x, KyberSwap, Barter, Portals, OKX DEX, DFlow, Hyperliquid, and Enso. [Velvet](https://www.velvet.capital/) [Velvet Docs](https://docs.velvet.capital/)

The product pitch is credible: one terminal for discovery, charts, routing, perps, yield, AI-assisted research, and trade preparation. Those are project-reported metrics and claims, but they are more substantive than the average AI-token launch. [Velvet](https://www.velvet.capital/)

## Research Question and Investment Relevance

The useful question is:

**Can Velvet turn AI-assisted onchain trading activity into durable token demand, or is VELVET mainly a launch-cycle proxy for an otherwise useful trading product?**

This matters because "AI trading" is often too vague to underwrite. Velvet is more concrete than that. It is not just an AI chatbot with a token; it is a trading terminal with routing, charts, non-custodial execution, perps through Hyperliquid, yield discovery, and a copilot called Velvet Unicorn. The issue is not product existence. The issue is token value capture.

| Layer | Product Question | Token Question |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Trading terminal | Do users trade through Velvet repeatedly? | Do fees create VELVET demand? |
| AI copilot | Does AI improve discovery or execution? | Is AI access tied to staking or usage? |
| Multi-chain routing | Does Velvet reduce workflow friction? | Does routing volume accrue to token holders? |
| Cashback | Does it retain traders? | Is cashback sustainable or subsidy-driven? |
| Vaults | Do managers and followers use onchain vaults? | Does VELVET capture vault AUM / fees? |

## Project Overview

Velvet started from DeFi portfolio and vault tooling and now positions itself as an AI onchain trading terminal. The docs describe four core surfaces:

| Product Surface | Description | Investment Read |
| --- | --- | --- |
| AI framework | AI-assisted research, analysis, and trade preparation | useful UX if it reduces trader search cost |
| Trading terminal | spot, perps, yield, charts, token discovery | strongest current product surface |
| Portfolio management / vaults | onchain vaults with manager/follower execution | potential asset-management wedge |
| Custom app / white-label infra | branded DeFi apps for projects and funds | B2B optionality |

The terminal supports seven chains, routes through many DEX and liquidity sources, and is non-custodial. Velvet says users keep control of assets, do not deposit funds into a centralized account, and approve every onchain transaction from their wallet. [Velvet](https://www.velvet.capital/) [Velvet Docs](https://docs.velvet.capital/)

The product also leans into "Trenches" style discovery: newly launched tokens from Pump.fun, Clanker, Bonk, Meteora, Printr, Bankr, and similar venues. That is important because active retail traders often value speed and discovery more than portfolio analytics.

## Value Capture and Token Utility

Velvet's token value-capture question is unresolved.

The official site says Velvet charges trading fees rather than subscriptions, offers up to **100% cashback** based on trading volume and **VELVET staking**, gives new users 15% cashback during their first active month, gives referred users 20% in their first month, and pays referrers 50% of referred users' trading fees for life. [Velvet](https://www.velvet.capital/)

That creates a plausible token loop:

1. More traders use Velvet.
2. More trades produce platform fees.
3. VELVET staking improves cashback or fee outcomes.
4. Traders stake or hold VELVET to reduce net trading cost.
5. If retention is real, staking demand supports token demand.

The open questions are more important:

| Question | Why It Matters |
| --- | --- |
| What is the actual take rate? | determines fee pool size |
| How much volume is organic after cashback? | separates PMF from subsidy |
| What share of fees is retained versus rebated? | determines economics |
| Is staking lockup meaningful or short-term? | affects float and sell pressure |
| Do vaults create AUM-based fees? | could add a second revenue line |
| Are token holders entitled to revenue? | absent this, token is utility/discount, not cash-flow claim |

Until those answers are visible, VELVET should not be valued like protocol equity.

## Competitive Landscape

Velvet competes with multiple categories at once:

| Competitor Set | Examples | Velvet Advantage | Velvet Weakness |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| DEX aggregators | 1inch, Matcha, Jupiter | broader terminal + AI layer | aggregators have deeper routing brand |
| Onchain trading terminals | Dexscreener, Birdeye, Photon-style tools | trading, AI, perps, yield in one app | trader habits are sticky |
| Perp venues | Hyperliquid, GMX, Drift | can aggregate rather than compete directly | perps value may accrue elsewhere |
| Vault platforms | Enzyme, dHEDGE, Superform-like surfaces | AI + terminal + vault combined | needs proven AUM |
| AI trading tools | bots, signal tools, agents | non-custodial execution and routing | AI quality hard to verify |

The bull case is that Velvet becomes the "Bloomberg-ish" onchain terminal for active retail and semi-professional traders. The bear case is that users keep using specialized tools: Dexscreener for discovery, Jupiter/1inch for swaps, Hyperliquid for perps, and wallet-native UX for execution.

## Verdict

Velvet is a **selective AI / DeFi trading-infrastructure watchlist**, not a high-conviction allocation yet.

The positive case is tangible. Velvet has a real product surface, non-custodial execution, seven-chain support, AI copilot positioning, onchain discovery, perps through Hyperliquid, yield routing, vault tooling, notable backers, audit claims, and visible Base / BSC liquidity. That is a better setup than most "AI crypto" tokens.

The caution is equally clear. VELVET is not automatically a claim on Velvet's business. The token still needs to prove durable utility through staking, fee rebates, cashback economics, vault AUM, and trader retention. If the token is mostly a discount asset with high incentives, the market cap can decouple from fundamentals quickly.

My current view: **watchlist, not core allocation.** VELVET becomes more compelling if the project reports recurring monthly volume, net fees after cashback, active trader retention, staking participation, and vault AUM, while the Base VELVET/USDC pool deepens above $10M. Without those signals, the product can keep improving while the token remains a high-beta AI / DeFi narrative asset.
