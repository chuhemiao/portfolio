## Lawsuit Dismissal: A Landmark Win for DeFi

On March 3, 2026 (Beijing time early hours, ~March 2 US time), U.S. District Judge Katherine Polk Failla dismissed the Risley v. Universal Navigation Inc. class action with prejudice—meaning no refiling on the same claims.

Plaintiffs alleged Uniswap Labs, founder Hayden Adams, and VCs (Paradigm, a16z) facilitated "rug pulls" and pump-and-dumps for 38 scam tokens traded on Uniswap from 2021-2022. Key rulings:

- Uniswap's permissionless, decentralized nature prevents control over listings or users—likened to blaming self-driving car developers for crimes committed with the vehicle.
- No proof of Uniswap's prior knowledge of specific scams or substantial assistance; indirect benefits (e.g., user growth) deemed too speculative for unjust enrichment.
- Prior 2023 federal securities dismissal upheld on appeal (2025), with state claims now fully rejected.

Impact: Removes major legal risk, setting precedent that DeFi developers aren't liable for third-party misuse of open-source code. Uniswap's Brian Nistler called it "precedent-setting for DeFi"; Adams tweeted it's a "fair outcome" pinning blame on scammers. [CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/policy/2026/03/03/scam-token-case-against-uniswap-dismissed-with-prejudice-by-u-s-district-judge-in-nyc) UNI surged ~6% to $3.92-$3.97 intraday March 3, with social buzz (Santiment noted UNI trending alongside BTC/AAVE).
